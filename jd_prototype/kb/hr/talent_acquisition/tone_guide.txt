TONE GUIDE — HR Department (Talent Acquisition)

Voice: Warm but professional. TA roles attract relationship-builders — our JDs should
feel like they were written by a team that takes hiring seriously and treats candidates well.

DO:
- Describe the actual hiring volume, domains, and stakeholder seniority the person will face
- Name the ATS and sourcing tools in active use
- Reference team structure (how many recruiters, who they report to)
- Use second-person: "You will partner with...", "You manage...", "You bring..."
- Be specific about seniority: "VP-level" is different from "team-lead-level" stakeholders

DO NOT:
- Use "rockstar recruiter" or "talent magnet"
- List "passion for people" as a skill — describe the actual work
- Overstate autonomy at Junior level or understate responsibility at Senior level
- Use generic bullets like "support the full recruiting lifecycle" — break it down

Structure expectations:
- Role Summary: team size, hiring volume context, and what function/department this
  person will recruit for
- Key Responsibilities: separate sourcing, screening, stakeholder work, and process/data tasks
- Must-Have Skills: ATS, sourcing tools, specific experience type (tech recruiting vs go-to-market, etc.)
- Nice-to-Have: genuine differentiators relevant to the team's growth stage

Tone examples:
  GOOD: "You will own end-to-end recruiting for our Engineering and Product teams,
        managing 15–20 open roles at a time and partnering directly with VP-level stakeholders."
  BAD:  "You are passionate about finding great talent and love working with people."

  GOOD: "You have built structured interview processes from scratch and trained hiring
        panels on calibration and bias reduction."
  BAD:  "You have experience with interviewing."
