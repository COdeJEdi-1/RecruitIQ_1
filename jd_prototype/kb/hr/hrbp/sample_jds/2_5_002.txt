◆  ROLE SUMMARY
──────────────────────────────────────────────
You will own the HR business partnering function for our GCC (Global Capability Centre) team, working closely with the TA Manager and internal stakeholders to drive business outcomes. As an HRBP, you will partner with the GCC leadership team to develop and implement HR strategies that align with the organization's goals. You will be responsible for driving HR initiatives and providing support to a team of approximately 200 employees. Your primary focus will be on GCC hiring, ensuring that the organization attracts and retains top talent.

◆  KEY RESPONSIBILITIES
──────────────────────────────────────────────
•  Deliver HR business partnering services to the GCC team, including employee relations, grievance handling, and performance management.
•  Own the implementation of HR policies and procedures, ensuring compliance with organizational standards and regulatory requirements.
•  Coordinate with the recruitment team to develop and implement hiring strategies that meet the GCC's talent acquisition needs.
•  Build and maintain relationships with internal stakeholders, including managers and employees, to provide guidance on HR-related matters.
•  Improve HR processes and systems, including the implementation of new HR technologies and tools, such as Darwinbox.
•  Partner with the learning and development team to design and deliver training programs that support the GCC's business objectives.
•  Analyze HR data and metrics, including turnover, absenteeism, and employee engagement, to inform HR strategies and initiatives.
•  Develop and maintain HR reports and dashboards to track key performance indicators and provide insights to stakeholders.

◆  MUST-HAVE SKILLS
──────────────────────────────────────────────
•  Darwinbox — HRMS system management, including data management, reporting, and analytics.
•  Business partnering for a functional unit — experience working with senior leaders to develop and implement HR strategies.
•  Employee relations & grievance handling — expertise in resolving employee conflicts and grievances.
•  Performance management — knowledge of performance management frameworks and processes.
•  Workforce planning — experience with workforce planning tools and techniques, including forecasting and succession planning.
•  HR policy implementation — expertise in developing and implementing HR policies and procedures.
•  Microsoft Office — proficiency in Microsoft Office applications, including Excel, Word, and PowerPoint.

◆  GOOD TO HAVE
──────────────────────────────────────────────
•  Certification in HR, such as SHRM-CP or PHR — demonstrating expertise in HR principles and practices.
•  Experience with HR analytics and reporting tools, such as Tableau or Power BI — enabling data-driven decision-making.
•  Knowledge of Indian labor laws and regulations, including the Shops and Establishments Act — ensuring compliance with regulatory requirements.
•  Experience working in a fast-paced, dynamic environment, with a focus on technology and innovation — adapting to changing business needs.
