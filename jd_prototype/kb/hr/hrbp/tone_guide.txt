TONE GUIDE — HR Department (HRBP)

Voice: Credible and grounded. HRBP candidates are evaluating whether this company
takes HR seriously as a strategic function. The JD should reflect that.

DO:
- Name the business unit or function this HRBP will support
- State the approximate headcount they will own
- Describe the maturity of the HR function (building from scratch vs established team)
- Use second-person: "You will partner with...", "You own...", "You bring..."
- Be honest about the mix of strategic vs operational work

DO NOT:
- Use "people person" or "culture champion" as descriptors
- List "passion for people" — describe the actual scope of influence
- Overstate how strategic the role is if it includes significant transactional work
- Be vague about seniority of business stakeholders (say VP-level, not just "senior leaders")

Structure expectations:
- Role Summary: business unit, headcount, HRBP team size, and reporting line
- Key Responsibilities: balance strategic work (OD, talent planning) with operational
  work (ER, performance cycles) accurately
- Must-Have Skills: ER depth, HRIS proficiency, and a specific domain (e.g. tech business
  partnering or commercial partnering)
- Nice-to-Have: qualifications and specialist experience relevant to this hire

Tone examples:
  GOOD: "You will own the people agenda for our 200-person Engineering organisation,
        partnering directly with the CTO and four VPs of Engineering."
  BAD:  "You will be a trusted partner to our business leaders."

  GOOD: "You have managed complex employee relations cases, including restructures and
        performance improvement plans, without close guidance."
  BAD:  "You have experience in employee relations."
