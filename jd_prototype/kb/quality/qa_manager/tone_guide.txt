TONE GUIDE — Quality Assurance Department (QA Manager) · Arvind Limited

Voice: Precise, test-method-specific, and buyer-aware. QA candidates in textiles
know every certification acronym and will dismiss any JD that uses vague quality
language. Name the test methods, the inspection systems, and the buyer names.

DO:
- Name test standards by code: ASTM D5034, ISO 13937, AATCC 61 — don't just say "testing experience"
- Reference the 4-point inspection system by name at all levels
- Name buyer quality requirements: H&M Way, M&S P&Q, VF Corp Restricted Substances List
- Reference Arvind's certifications in context: OEKO-TEX 100, GOTS, Bluesign, ZDHC
- Use second-person: "You will own...", "You will lead...", "You will manage..."
- Specify team size for mid-level and above

DO NOT:
- Use "attention to detail" as a skill — it's assumed for every QA role
- Say "quality experience" without specifying fabric type (woven, knit, denim) and process (lab, inline, final)
- Overstate Six Sigma requirements at junior level
- Use "zero defect" without quantifying the target (e.g., <500 PPM)

Structure expectations:
- Role Summary: Arvind plant context, fabric category (denim/woven/technical), inspection scope (inline/final/lab), and team size
- Key Responsibilities: inspection tasks + audit management + certification maintenance + data reporting
- Must-Have Skills: inspection system + test standards + AQL knowledge + buyer audit experience at appropriate depth
- Nice-to-Have: Six Sigma belt, third-party lab accreditation, chemical testing (RSL/MRSL)

Tone examples:
  GOOD: "You will manage inline and final inspection for Arvind's denim plant in Santej,
        overseeing a team of 8 QC inspectors, owning AQL Level II sampling plans, and
        conducting weekly quality reviews with the Plant Head."
  BAD:  "You will ensure quality standards are maintained across the production floor."

  GOOD: "You have hands-on experience with the 4-point inspection system for woven
        fabrics and have handled buyer audits (H&M or M&S) as the primary QA contact."
  BAD:  "You have experience in quality control and understand industry standards."
