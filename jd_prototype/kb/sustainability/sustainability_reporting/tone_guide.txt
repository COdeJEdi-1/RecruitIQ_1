TONE GUIDE — Sustainability / ESG Department · Arvind Limited

Voice: Credible, data-driven, and textile-industry-specific. Sustainability candidates
in the apparel/textile space know the difference between generic ESG and real
supply chain sustainability. Name the frameworks, name the buyer requirements, name
the Arvind-specific programs.

DO:
- Reference specific frameworks: GRI, BRSR, TCFD, GHG Protocol, SBTi
- Name textile-specific certifications and standards: OEKO-TEX, GOTS, ZDHC MRSL, BCI (Better Cotton)
- Mention buyer sustainability requirements by name (H&M Higg index, PVH Corp standards, VF Corp)
- Reference Arvind's water stewardship program and SEBI BRSR Core compliance
- Use second-person: "You will own...", "You will lead...", "You will build..."
- Be specific about data scope: plant-level vs group-level, Scope 1/2/3

DO NOT:
- Use "passionate about sustainability" — describe the actual work
- List BRSR or GRI as a nice-to-have for any level beyond junior — these are baseline
- Confuse ESG reporting with CSR/philanthropy — Arvind's sustainability work is operational
- Use vague language like "coordinate with teams" — name the teams (plant EHS, supply chain, finance)

Structure expectations:
- Role Summary: Arvind's sustainability scope, reporting framework ownership, team or committee context
- Key Responsibilities: data collection, report writing, stakeholder engagement, and certification management — in proportion to the experience level
- Must-Have Skills: framework knowledge at the appropriate depth, data tools, and Arvind-specific standards
- Nice-to-Have: advanced tools (Watershed, Sweep), SBTi, green finance

Tone examples:
  GOOD: "You will own Arvind's Scope 1 and Scope 2 emissions data consolidation across
        six manufacturing plants, prepare the GHG Protocol inventory, and support
        the BRSR Core disclosure for the listed entity."
  BAD:  "You will work on sustainability initiatives and support reporting activities."

  GOOD: "You have managed ZDHC MRSL compliance data for a textile manufacturer and
        understand the difference between RSL testing and MRSL verification at
        chemical supplier level."
  BAD:  "You have an interest in sustainability and some reporting experience."
