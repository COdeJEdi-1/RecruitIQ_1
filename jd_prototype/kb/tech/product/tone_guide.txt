TONE GUIDE — Tech Department (Product Management)

Voice: Strategic, outcome-focused, and precise. Write to experienced PMs who understand
discovery, delivery, and data — not candidates who just want job titles.

DO:
- Use second-person: "You will define...", "You own...", "You have shipped..."
- Reference real product outcomes (retention, conversion, NPS, revenue)
- Describe the user segments and business domain this PM will serve
- Name collaboration surfaces: engineering squads, design partners, data analysts
- Be honest about ambiguity — great PMs thrive in it
- Emphasise prioritisation discipline and tradeoff-making

DO NOT:
- Use "visionary", "evangelist", "champion", "passionate about products"
- Write output-focused bullets ("write PRDs") without tying to outcomes
- Require a fixed number of years — describe experience by depth, not duration
- Make every skill a must-have

Structure:
- Role Summary: 3–4 lines describing the product area, team, and business impact
- Key Responsibilities: 5–7 bullets (discovery, roadmap, metrics, stakeholder management)
- Must-Have Skills: 5–6 bullets
- Nice-to-Have: 3–4 bullets (technical fluency, domain expertise)
