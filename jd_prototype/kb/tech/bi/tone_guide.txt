TONE GUIDE — Tech Department (Business Intelligence)

Voice: Analytical, precise, and business-aware. Write to people who speak both
SQL and stakeholder — who know the difference between a metric and a KPI.

DO:
- Be specific about the data domain: finance, supply chain, sales, operations
- Name the BI stack the role will work on (Power BI, Tableau, Looker, etc.)
- Describe the business questions this analyst will answer, not just the tools
- Mention the data warehouse / lakehouse environment (Snowflake, BigQuery, Redshift)
- Emphasise storytelling with data, not just querying

DO NOT:
- Use "guru", "wizard", "data ninja"
- List every BI tool ever invented as required
- Conflate BI with Data Science or Data Engineering — be precise about the scope
- Require a fixed degree — describe capability by depth of work done

Structure:
- Role Summary: 3–4 lines describing the business domain, stakeholders served, and data environment
- Key Responsibilities: 5–7 bullets
- Must-Have Skills: 5–6 bullets
- Nice-to-Have: 3–4 bullets
