TONE GUIDE — Tech Department (Data Science & Machine Learning)

Voice: Rigorous, intellectually honest, and impact-oriented. Write to practitioners
who distinguish between a model and a product — who know deployment is half the work.

DO:
- Describe the actual problem domain (demand forecasting, recommendation, NLP, CV, etc.)
- Name the data environment and model serving infrastructure
- Distinguish between research-heavy and production-heavy roles clearly
- Reference the business impact — not just model accuracy but business metrics moved
- Acknowledge the full ML lifecycle: data, modelling, evaluation, deployment, monitoring

DO NOT:
- Use "AI wizard", "ML guru", "passionate about AI"
- Require a PhD unless it's genuinely necessary — describe depth of work instead
- List every ML framework ever invented as required
- Confuse Data Science with Data Engineering or BI

Structure:
- Role Summary: 3–4 lines describing the problem space, team, and production context
- Key Responsibilities: 5–7 bullets
- Must-Have Skills: 5–6 bullets
- Nice-to-Have: 3–4 bullets
