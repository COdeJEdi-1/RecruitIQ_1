TONE GUIDE — Tech Department (Software Development Engineer — Full Stack / SDE)

Voice: Direct, specific, and collegial. Write to engineers as peers, not candidates.
Describe real work, real systems, and real trade-offs — not aspirational fluff.

DO:
- Use second-person: "You will own...", "You have built...", "You are comfortable..."
- Name specific technologies — vague language signals a poor JD
- Describe end-to-end ownership (frontend, backend, or both as the role demands)
- Reference actual systems, scale, or product surfaces this engineer will touch
- Keep bullets tight and scannable — one sentence each
- Use active verbs: own, build, ship, design, review, instrument, migrate

DO NOT:
- Use "rockstar", "ninja", "guru", "full-stack wizard", "passionate"
- Write vague bullets like "work across the stack" without specifics
- List more than 6 must-haves
- Inflate seniority

Structure:
- Role Summary: 3–4 lines describing team, product, and what makes this role interesting
- Key Responsibilities: 5–7 bullets (real tasks)
- Must-Have Skills: 5–6 bullets
- Nice-to-Have: 3–4 bullets
