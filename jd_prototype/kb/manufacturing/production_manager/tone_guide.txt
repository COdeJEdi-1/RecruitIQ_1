TONE GUIDE — Manufacturing Department (Production Manager) · Arvind Limited

Voice: Direct, floor-grounded, and technically precise. Production candidates evaluate
whether the role is real — vague JDs signal an organisation that doesn't understand
its own operations. Name machines, name targets, name the plant context.

DO:
- Reference specific loom types (rapier, airjet, projectile), fabric types (denim, woven), and processes (sizing, warping, finishing)
- State shift targets in metres/shift or machine efficiency (OEE %) wherever possible
- Name the plant geography where relevant (Gujarat, Karnataka, Jharkhand, Maharashtra)
- Use second-person: "You will plan...", "You will own...", "You will drive..."
- Be honest about the leadership scope — state team size and reporting line
- Reference Arvind's sustainability commitments (OEKO-TEX, ISO 14001) for mid+ levels

DO NOT:
- Use abstract corporate language — this is a plant floor role
- Say "fast-paced environment" — every textile plant is fast-paced
- Overstate autonomy for junior roles or understate accountability for senior ones
- List certifications (ISO, OEKO-TEX) as must-haves at junior level

Structure expectations:
- Role Summary: name the plant or department, the fabric category, the scale (number of machines or metres/day)
- Key Responsibilities: split between daily operations, improvement initiatives, and people/stakeholder work
- Must-Have Skills: loom/machine-specific + OEE/5S + reporting tool (SAP PP, Excel)
- Nice-to-Have: automation, sustainability certs, Lean/Six Sigma at junior; capex, IR at senior

Tone examples:
  GOOD: "You will manage weaving operations across 120 rapier looms, owning shift efficiency
        targets of 82% OEE and leading a team of 25 operators and 3 shift supervisors."
  BAD:  "You will work in a dynamic textile manufacturing environment."

  GOOD: "You have 5S certification and have implemented Kaizen projects that improved
        fabric yield by 3%+ in a previous plant role."
  BAD:  "You have experience in manufacturing and are detail-oriented."
