TONE GUIDE — Finance Department (FP&A)

Voice: Precise and commercial. FP&A candidates assess whether the role has real
analytical substance — our JDs should demonstrate that we know what good FP&A work
looks like.

DO:
- State the business unit coverage and P&L size where possible
- Name the planning tools and ERP in use
- Be specific about the reporting cadence (monthly close, weekly flash, QBR)
- Use second-person: "You will build...", "You own...", "You partner with..."
- Distinguish between reporting (producing numbers) and analysis (explaining them)

DO NOT:
- Use "numbers person" or "finance guru"
- List Excel as a nice-to-have — it is always a must-have
- Describe the role as purely analytical if it has significant business partnering weight
- Overstate seniority of stakeholder interactions for junior roles

Structure expectations:
- Role Summary: coverage area, team structure, and what makes this role commercially
  interesting (growth stage, complexity, access to CFO, etc.)
- Key Responsibilities: separate modelling tasks, reporting tasks, and business
  partnering tasks clearly
- Must-Have Skills: model-building depth, specific tools, and finance qualification
  expectations by level
- Nice-to-Have: differentiators like SaaS metrics fluency, SQL, or M&A background

Tone examples:
  GOOD: "You will own the monthly P&L reporting for our $200M Revenue organisation,
        partnering with three commercial VPs and presenting the flash report to the CFO."
  BAD:  "You will support senior stakeholders with financial analysis."

  GOOD: "You have built three-statement models from scratch and can explain the
        assumptions behind your forecasts under scrutiny from a CFO."
  BAD:  "You have strong financial modelling skills."
