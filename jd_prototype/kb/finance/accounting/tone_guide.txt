TONE GUIDE — Finance Department (Accounting)

Voice: Clear, rigorous, and process-oriented. Accounting candidates value accuracy
and clarity — a JD with vague scope or inflated seniority signals a disorganised finance function.

DO:
- State the close timeline and what the candidate will own within it
- Name the ERP and reconciliation tools in active use
- Be clear about whether this is a standalone role or part of a team
- Use second-person: "You will own...", "You prepare...", "You partner with..."
- Be honest about audit involvement (prep vs primary contact vs liaison)

DO NOT:
- Use "detail-oriented" as a must-have — it is a given for all accounting roles
- List more skills than are genuinely required for this level
- Overstate strategic involvement for individual contributor accounting roles
- Describe a Senior role as a "support" role or a Junior role as "leading" the close

Structure expectations:
- Role Summary: company stage (early-stage, scaling, public), team size, close cadence,
  and what the candidate will own
- Key Responsibilities: close tasks, reporting tasks, and cross-functional work in proportion
  to the actual role split
- Must-Have Skills: close experience, ERP proficiency, and qualification expectations by level
- Nice-to-Have: technical accounting, public-company experience, or specialist areas

Tone examples:
  GOOD: "You will own the fixed assets and prepaid expense subledgers, posting all entries
        and preparing the supporting schedules reviewed by the Controller during our 5-day close."
  BAD:  "You will support the accounting team with month-end close tasks."

  GOOD: "You have prepared reconciliations that have gone through external audit without
        material adjustments and can explain your methodology under scrutiny."
  BAD:  "You have experience with reconciliations and are detail-oriented."
